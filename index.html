Projects

Monetary Independence Assistant

MIA is a personal finance app designed specifically for young adults, with over five months of research, testing, ideation, and implementation, I created a user-friendly app that aims to assist users in gaining control of their financial lives and making informed decisions. MIA serves as a hub for managing income, bills, goals, and aims to empower young adults to take the first steps towards complete financial independence.

GV Design Sprint (PostUp)

A Google Ventures-style design sprint for the app "PostUp," which aims to connect busy workers with public workspaces where they can complete their tasks efficiently. As the sole designer, I was presented with a user research brief and a problem to be solved. Utilizing a structured design process, I was able to develop and prototype potential solutions to the identified problem.

Blink Bikes

Blink Bikes was designed as an exclusive e-commerce website that specializes in selling high-end bicycles to high earning clients. After being presented with a problem to solve, I utilized an organized and iterative design process with a primary focus on providing a cohesive shopping experience and an extensive range of premium bikes to choose from. The website caters to the needs of those seeking an elevated cycling experience.

About Me

As a skilled UX designer and developer, I have the ability to take a product from conception through ideation, testing, and design to a live code prototype. My knowledge of the principles of design, collegiate studies in psychology, and frontend expertise allows me to approach projects from a holistic perspective. In my work, I strive to simplify processes as much as possible, and I take pride in demonstrating this through my designs.

I completed one year as a psychology student at Penn State before deciding to take a gap year to explore other fields of study. During this time, I discovered my passion for UI Design and pursued it through Springboard. I am now close to completing the program and excited to apply my skills to real-world projects.

My immediate plan is to search for job opportunities to jumpstart my career. However, I am also open to the possibility of pursuing further education part-time while working full-time, or vice versa. If a company is willing to provide financial support for my education, I would be eager to take advantage of that opportunity.
